## 🧭 Project Overview

Savills provided a dataset of **3,029 commercial lease transactions** representing over **174 million square feet** of office space across U.S. markets from 2018–2024. The COVID-19 pandemic fundamentally disrupted the commercial real estate sector, and Savills needed data-driven advice to guide clients on **where, when, and how to lease office space** as the market recovers.

Our team structured the analysis around four core business questions:

> **Who's moving? Where to lease? How to lease? What's next?**

---

## 🔍 Key Business Questions

1. Which industry sectors are driving leasing activity post-COVID?
2. Which cities offer the most robust leasing opportunities?
3. What percentage of high-quality (Class A) buildings are available?
4. How do space types and lease strategies differ across markets?
5. What do short-term and long-term forecasts signal for key markets?

---

## 💡 Key Findings

**What industry to target:**
- **Tech sector** dominates leasing volume — high-volume, trend-setting, and actively reshaping post-COVID patterns
- Tech leasing signals are the strongest leading indicator for market direction

**Where to lease:**
- **Atlanta** — most resilient market: strong infrastructure + cost advantage = best investment target
- **Austin** — active leasing activity with relet-heavy supply (ready-to-use spaces)
- **San Francisco** — oversupply risk; enter only through discounted sublets
- **New York** — softening conditions; prioritize sublets in prime locations, delay expansion

**How to lease:**
- Atlanta & Austin: Focus on well-located, move-in-ready spaces
- New York: Prioritize sublets, delay long-term commitments
- San Francisco: Use discounted sublets as entry strategy

**Forecasting:**
- Short-term: Atlanta shows resilience; San Francisco carries high oversupply risk
- Long-term: Tenant-favorable conditions in sublet-rich cities signal opportunity for flexible leases
